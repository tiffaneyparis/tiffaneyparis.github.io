  • Calvin Teoh

    I enjoy the simple monochromatic style of this site. It's classic, elegant, and not overly complicated. It stays blue throughout, until clicking the links, then it comes to life by adding the proper, vibrant color.

  • Keep Portland Weird

    I appreciate the original drawings on this one, almost like a cut and paste and cartoon feel. After entering the site, rows of boxes with muted colors show a representation of the alphabet, keeping with the almost elementary style. They have simple illustrations, and it draws you in to wanting to know what's behind each box.

  • Heck House

    This site has a retro feel at first glance, with the black and white intro page. The navigation is easy and not too complicated. Scrolling down brings up colored boxes with small animations, to keep you interested in clicking them. Clicking on the links brings more great design element, and stays somewhat uniform. The pictures are a bit on the large side, but doesn't take too long to load.

  • Enso Magazine Article

    What I really like the way this site is layed out. The odd typography grabs you, and as you scroll down scrolling alternates between horizontal and back down again. The pictures are bright and clear, and beautiful. The layout is arranged in a uniform and interesting way. Also, the dropdown menu on the right lets you skip ahead to pick where you want to go in the navigation.

  • Fixed Group

    I really enjoy the youthfulness of this site. It's not only professional, but inovative with an inspired layout. On the portfolio site, the boxes seem to rotate around like a ferris wheel. The colors are vibrant and it's easy to navigate.
